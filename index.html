About

Who I Am

I'm a hardware engineer specializing in FPGA design for space systems, currently at Astranis Space Technologies building satellites for high orbits. My work lives at the intersection of digital logic, embedded systems, and the extreme demands of the space environment.

Before Astranis, I spent a year at Blue Origin as an FPGA Engineer on the Lunar Permanence program, and before that at Intellian Technologies designing RTL logic for user terminals (one of the earth-dwelling components of a satellite network). My career started at Cobham Advanced Electronic Solutions (CAES) designing PCB's for defense applications. At CAES I learned the fundamentals of PCB design and debug.

I earned my B.S. in Electrical & Computer Engineering from Temple University and am now pursuing my M.S. in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Johns Hopkins Whiting School of Engineering.
